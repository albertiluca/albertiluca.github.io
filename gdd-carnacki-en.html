1. Basics & Concept

Prompt & High-Concept: Official video game adaptation of William Hope Hodgson's (1877-1918) novel and short stories series, "Carnacki: The Ghost Finder". The title is conceived as a 3D first-person psychological horror experience with a strong investigative component, characterized by noir, thriller, and Lovecraftian tones.

Game Structure: The gameplay is strictly linear, focusing on closed scenarios and well-defined paths to maximize the dramatic pacing of emotional tension. The campaign offers an immersive single-player experience, supported by achievement-unlock logic tied to different game difficulties.

Visual Style & Art Direction: The project adopts a hybrid storytelling style designed to optimize production costs and maximize immersion in the late-Victorian / Edwardian atmosphere (1905-1912):

  • Comic Cinematic (The Frame): Narrative sequences designed as Motion Comics. The illustrations reproduce the charcoal and ink sketch style from the historical production documents, partially animated with parallax, smoke, and dynamic rain effects for transition scenes.
  • 3D Gameplay: Active exploration in first person, characterized by dim diegetic lighting, oppressive rooms, and cinematic sound design.

Inspirations & Benchmark: Riven, The Shore, Call of Cthulhu: Dark Corners of the Earth, The Sinking City, What Remains of Edith Finch, The Vanishing of Ethan Carter.

2. Core Gameplay Loop & Mechanics

The main interaction loop is divided into two recurring systemic phases: Investigation and Ritual Siege.

A. Investigative Phase & Exploration

  • Psychophysical Status Monitoring (ECG): Carnacki's emotional stress is constantly monitored via a diegetic electrocardiograph integrated into the game journal. In the presence of anomalies or paranormal entities, the heart rate increases progressively. High stress causes motor instability (aim sway) and slows down interaction times with objects. The player must consume alchemical extracts to calm the nervous system.
  • Spirit Photography: A fundamental analytical tool for detecting traces invisible to the naked eye. The player must physically place the camera on a stable tripod, expose a plate sensitive to esoteric frequencies, and develop the negatives using chemical solvents to reveal occult clues, runes, or entities' weak points.
  • Inventory Management (Radial Wheel): The player has a tool wheel with a limit of 8 slots. Key resources (batteries, ammunition, chalk, extracts) are limited, forcing the player into careful strategic planning before leaving safe rooms.

B. Ritual Siege Phase

  • Defense Building (Planning): The player must physically set up protective barriers in the investigation environment, following three sequential operational steps:
    1. Cleaning: Use of the Hyssop Broom to eliminate spiritual contamination from the tracking area.
    2. Drawing: Drawing the primary protective circle on the floor using chalk or salt (featuring a dynamic wear system of the perimeter).
    3. Electrification: Advanced placement of vacuum tubes and Weston Cells to electrically charge the ritual circle.
  • Strategic Combat (The 3 Phases):
    1. Repulsion: Frequency modulation of the Electric Pentacle's force field to stop enemy charges.
    2. Traps: Use of firearms to shoot environmental accumulators or external switches, releasing protective electric arcs.
    3. Confinement: High-risk phase where the player must temporarily exit the safety of the circle to physically engrave the Saamaaa Seals onto the entity's anchor, closing the dimensional rift permanently.

3. World Architecture & Level Design

The game world is structured into three linear macro-scenarios designed to support the narrative and emphasize the cyclical flow of the chapters:

  • 1. 427 Cheyne Walk (Chelsea, London): Carnacki's private residence. Serves as the central hub and safe haven for narrative transition segments before starting missions.
  • 2. The Anderson Mansion: The main scenario of the first case. An isolated, ghostly, and oppressive mansion, where closed spaces (specifically the Gray Room) are designed as arenas for strategic defense and environmental puzzles.
  • 3. The Gut (London): Urban transition area. Allows the player to interface with shopkeepers and artisans to purchase chemical components, supply Weston cells, and develop photographic plates.

4. Folklore, Lore & Spells

The magical and scientific cosmology of the game relies entirely on the mysterious and esoteric Sigsand Manuscript (14th century), the only theoretical resource in Carnacki's possession to counter external threats.

  • The Saamaaa Ritual: Composed of 8 core signs that grant physical and mental barriers (Protection, Projection, Sharing, Observation, Force, Opportunity, Clarity, Power). The correct sequential order is vital for activating defensive systems.
  • The Seven Planes Theory:
    • Lower Planes (1-4): Malkuth (Elemental), Netzach (Terrestrial), Hod (Astral/Dreams), and Kethur (Causal). They constitute the interaction dimension where the gameplay takes place and from which ghostly manifestations emerge.
    • Upper Planes (5-7): Fohatic, Mahatic, and Alayic. Realms governed by pure mathematical laws and sources of positive energy.

5. Dramatis Personae (Characters)

  • Thomas Carnacki: Protagonist. Occult investigator, a meticulous and pragmatic man of science, guided by logic and respect for the laws of physics. He faces the unknown with the fear of one who understands its real threat, but with the determination of one who possesses the scientific and historical tools to counter it.
  • The Cheyne Walk Circle (Dodgson, Jessop, Arkwright, Taylor): Close friends of the protagonist. They constitute the social and narrative frame. Dodgson acts as the external narrator and represents, along with the others, the player's rational perspective on the horrors of the recalled cases.
  • Peter (The Butler of the Anderson Mansion): Key secondary character of Chapter 1. A fearful but loyal man. Custodian of the mansion's historical secrets, he introduces Carnacki to the mysteries of the residence and acts as an internal guide.
  • Miranda (The Maid of the Anderson Mansion): The newest addition to the mansion's staff. Quiet, tied to the Anderson family by a past moral debt that prevents her from leaving the estate despite the impending curse.

6. System Taxonomy (Enemies)

External paranormal threats (classified as Ab-natural) follow the strict categorization of the Sigsand Manuscript:

  • Class 1 — Psychic Leaks / Poltergeist: Acoustic or visual anomalies that do not cause direct biological damage, but undermine the protagonist's psychophysical stability by increasing heart rate (ECG).
  • Class 2 — Aeiirii (Ghosts/Demons): Semi-material manifestations bound to a physical anchor. They induce biological damage and psychosis, requiring protective barriers.
  • Class 3 & 4 — Saiitii (Moving Shadows): Energy parasites of dead organic or inorganic matter. Highly hostile and highly mobile, they can bypass or corrupt primary defenses. Fire is the only neutralizing agent.
  • Class 5 — Raaee (Astarri): Superior, benevolent, energy-charged light entities. They act by providing cryptographic suggestions or can be invoked via the Unknown Last Line as a saving resource in situations of extreme danger.
  • The Shadow Hand (Chapter 1 Boss): A hostile Class 2 manifestation (Aeiirii) bound to a lost Anderson family ring. It can teleport in the dark, confuse the player's mind, and strike with lightning speed. It is vulnerable to electricity, seals, and recited formulas.

7. Chapter 1: Quest Scripting Flow

The following section illustrates the logical progression architecture of the first episode's missions, structured as logical steps of ordered objectives for game code tracking:

[INTRODUCTION]
- Objective 0.1: Interact with Peter the butler at the mansion entrance.

[STAGE 1: PREPARATION AND SAFETY]
- Objective 1.1: Place 3 Saiitii Signs and 3 Saamaaa Symbols in your guest room.
- Objective 1.2: Retrieve the Tool Bag and equip the Inventory.
- Objective 1.3: Reach Peter the butler in the Parlor.

[STAGE 2: INVESTIGATING THE GRAY ROOM]
- Objective 2.1: Light the 6 diegetic candelabras to restore primary lighting.
- Objective 2.2: Inspect the Gray Room searching for infestation rifts.
- Objective 2.3: Apply sealing tape to the 3 windows and place 3 coordinated tripwires.
- Objective 2.4: Apply the Saiitii Seals to the 3 detected energy anchor points.

[STAGE 3: PERIMETER ISOLATION]
- Objective 3.1: Go out into the corridor and seal the 5 access doors to prevent psychic leaks.

[STAGE 4: ANALYZING CLUES]
- Objective 4.1: Return to the Gray Room and conduct thorough research on furniture and the bed.
- Objective 4.2: Conduct an inspection in the library to decipher the occult symbols of the investigation.
- Objective 4.3: Talk to the butler and the maid to gather historical information.

[STAGE 5: SPIRIT PHOTOGRAPHY]
- Objective 5.1: Retrieve the camera and place the tripod in front of the Gray Room entrance.
- Objective 5.2: Connect the auto-shutter mechanism to the door handle to capture passing entities.

[STAGE 6: RESUPPLY AND DEVELOPMENT]
- Objective 6.1: Reach the 'Safarà' shop in London's Gut to develop plates and purchase 5 chemical reagents.
- Objective 6.2: Return to the Anderson Mansion and prepare for the final siege.

[STAGE 7: THE NIGHT SIEGE]
- Objective 7.1: Seal yourself inside the Gray Room.
- Objective 7.2: Draw the first ritual defense line (chalk/salt circle).
- Objective 7.3: Set up the second defense line (batteries and vacuum tubes) and activate the Electric Pentacle.
- Objective 7.4: Survive the night siege of the Shadow Hand and repel its attacks.

[STAGE 8: CONFINEMENT AND CONCLUSION]
- Objective 8.1: Locate the origin point of the infestation in the Gray Room and retrieve the Anderson ring (anchor).
- Objective 8.2: Exit the Pentacle before the final manifestation of the entity and capture it.
- Objective 8.3: Gather Peter and Miranda and definitively destroy the ring using the blowtorch, breaking the curse.

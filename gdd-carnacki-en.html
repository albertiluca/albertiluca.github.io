1. Basics & Concept

Prompt & High-Concept: Classic literary-style video game adaptation. The title is designed as a 3D first-person psychological horror experience with a deep investigative focus, featuring noir style, thriller elements, and Lovecraftian tones.

Game Structure: The gameplay is strictly linear, focusing on closed scenarios and well-defined paths to optimize the dramatic pacing. The campaign offers an immersive single-player experience, with achievements tied to different game difficulties.

Visual & Artistic Direction: The game adopts a hybrid storytelling style to optimize production costs and maximize historical immersion (1905-1912):

  • Comic Cinematic (The Frame): Narrative sequences designed in Motion Comic style, featuring charcoal and ink illustrations, animated with parallax, smoke, and rain effects.
  • 3D Gameplay: First-person active exploration, characterized by dim diegetic lighting, oppressive rooms, and cinematic sound design.

2. Core Gameplay Loop & Mechanics

The main loop is divided into two recurring systemic phases: Investigation and Ritual Siege.

A. Investigation Phase & Exploration

  • Status Monitoring (ECG): The protagonist's stress is tracked in real-time by a diegetic electrocardiograph in the journal. Near entities, the heart rate increases, causing camera shake (unstable aim) and slowing down interactions. The player must use alchemical extracts to calm down.
  • Spirit Photography: Vital for revealing hidden traces. The player must set up the camera on a tripod, take a plate photo, and develop the negatives with chemicals to reveal hidden runes and weak points.

B. Ritual Siege Phase

  • Defense Building (Planning): The player must set up protective barriers following three steps: purification with the Hyssop broom, drawing the chalk/salt circle, and electrification using batteries and vacuum tubes.
  • Strategic Combat (3 Stages): Repelling entities with the Electric Pentacle, shooting external fuses/triggers to release defensive electric arcs, and manual confinement by engraving the Protection Seals.

3. World Architecture & Level Design

  • 1. The Investigator's Study: The protagonist's house, used as the main hub for story segments.
  • 2. The Period Residence: The first case location, designed as a strategic defense arena (The Gray Chamber).
  • 3. The Urban District: Urban transition area to buy special components and equipment.

4. Folklore, Lore & Spells

The esoterical cosmology of the game relies entirely on an Ancient Manuscript (14th century).

  • The Seals Ritual: Composed of 8 core signs that create physical and mental barriers. The sequential order is vital to activate defensive systems.

5. Dramatis Personae (Characters)

Characters: The Investigator (Pragmatic occult scientist), the circle of witnesses, and the residence servants (the Butler and the Maid).

6. System Taxonomy (Enemies)

Threats: Classified from level 1 to 5. Focus on the Dark Entity (Class 2), the anomaly of the Gray Chamber tied to a lost family ring and highly vulnerable to electricity.

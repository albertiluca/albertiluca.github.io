1. Basics & Concept

Prompt & High-Concept: Classic literary-style video game adaptation set in the Project Shadowseeker universe. The title is designed as a 3D first-person psychological horror experience with a deep investigative focus, featuring noir style, thriller elements, and Lovecraftian tones.

Game Structure: The gameplay is strictly linear, focusing on closed scenarios and well-defined paths to optimize the dramatic pacing. The campaign offers an immersive single-player experience, with achievements tied to different game difficulties.

Visual & Artistic Direction: The game adopts a hybrid storytelling style to optimize production costs and maximize historical immersion (1905-1912):

  • Comic Cinematic (The Frame): Narrative sequences designed in Motion Comic style, featuring charcoal and ink illustrations, animated with parallax, smoke, and rain effects.
  • 3D Gameplay: First-person active exploration, characterized by dim diegetic lighting, oppressive rooms, and cinematic sound design.

Inspirations & Benchmark: Riven, The Shore, Call of Cthulhu: Dark Corners of the Earth, The Sinking City, What Remains of Edith Finch, The Vanishing of Ethan Carter.

2. Core Gameplay Loop & Mechanics

The main loop is divided into two recurring systemic phases: Investigation and Ritual Siege.

A. Investigation Phase & Exploration

  • Status Monitoring (ECG): The protagonist's stress is tracked in real-time by a diegetic electrocardiograph in the journal. Near entities, the heart rate increases, causing camera shake (unstable aim) and slowing down interactions. The player must use alchemical extracts to calm down.
  • Spirit Photography: Vital for revealing hidden traces. The player must set up the camera on a tripod, take a plate photo, and develop the negatives with chemicals to reveal hidden runes and weak points.
  • Inventory Management (Radial Wheel): The player has a tool wheel with a limit of 8 slots. Key resources (batteries, ammunition, chalk, extracts) are limited, forcing the player into careful strategic planning before leaving safe rooms.

B. Ritual Siege Phase

  • Defense Building (Planning): The player must set up protective barriers following three steps: purification with the Hyssop broom, drawing the chalk/salt circle, and electrification using batteries and vacuum tubes.
  • Strategic Combat (3 Stages): Repelling entities with the Electric Pentacle, shooting external fuses/triggers to release defensive electric arcs, and manual confinement by engraving the Protection Seals.

4. Folklore, Lore & Spells

The esoterical cosmology of the game relies entirely on an Ancient Manuscript (14th century) used by the Investigator to counter external threats.

  • The Seals Ritual: Composed of 8 core signs that create physical and mental barriers (Protection, Projection, Sharing, Observation, Force, Opportunity, Clarity, Power). The sequential order is vital to activate defensive systems.
  • The Seven Planes Theory:
    • Lower Planes (1-4): Malkuth (Elemental), Netzach (Terrestrial), Hod (Astral/Dreams), and Kethur (Causal). They constitute the interaction dimension where the gameplay takes place and from which ghostly manifestations emerge.
    • Upper Planes (5-7): Fohatic, Mahatic, and Alayic. Realms governed by pure mathematical laws and sources of positive energy.

6. System Taxonomy (Enemies)

External threats (classified as Ab-natural) follow the strict taxonomy of the Ancient Manuscript:

  • Class 1 — Psychic Leaks / Poltergeist: Acoustic or visual anomalies that do not cause direct biological damage, but undermine the protagonist's psychophysical stability by increasing heart rate (ECG).
  • Class 2 — Aeiirii (Ghosts/Demons): Semi-material manifestations bound to a physical anchor. They induce biological damage and psychosis, requiring protective barriers.
  • Class 3 & 4 — Saiitii (Moving Shadows): Energy parasites of dead organic or inorganic matter. Highly hostile and highly mobile, they can bypass or corrupt primary defenses. Fire is the only neutralizing agent.
  • Class 5 — Raaee (Astarri): Superior, benevolent, energy-charged light entities. They act by providing cryptographic suggestions or can be invoked via the Unknown Last Line as a saving resource in situations of extreme danger.
  • The Dark Entity (Chapter 1 Boss): A hostile Class 2 manifestation bound to a lost family ring. It can teleport in the dark, confuse the player's mind, and strike with lightning speed. It is vulnerable to electricity, seals, and recited formulas.
